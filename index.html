Why Halal Dining is More Popular Than Ever

Halal food is more than a religious requirement — it reflects values like cleanliness, ethical sourcing, and humane slaughter practices. In the UK, halal food is a fast-growing industry. According to a study by the Halal Monitoring Committee and University of Huddersfield, halal meat contributes to approximately 15% of the UK’s meat and poultry sector, valued at about £1.7 billion. It’s projected to rise to nearly £2 billion by 2028.

  • More high-street chains like Nando’s and KFC now offer halal branches.

  • Increased demand for verified certification over unverified claims.

Spotting Authentic Halal Restaurants

Anyone can label food as halal, so how do you tell what’s legit? Use this checklist:

  • Is there an HMC or similar certificate clearly visible?
  • Does the restaurant name its supplier?
  • Is there proper segregation between halal and non-halal items?
  • What are reviews saying in Muslim Facebook groups or online forums?

Challenges to Watch For

Searching for halal options isn’t always smooth. You may face:

  • Inconsistent certification — there’s no unified UK halal body.
  • Risk of cross-contamination in mixed kitchens.
  • Fewer options in rural areas or small towns.
  • “Halal” claims without any actual verification — always double check.
